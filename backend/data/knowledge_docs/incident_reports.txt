INCIDENT REPORT: BF-FAN-12-INC-2024-003
Date: March 14, 2024 | Reported by: R. Sharma, Sr. Technician

EQUIPMENT: BF Fan 12
FAULT TYPE: Drive End Bearing Failure
SEVERITY: High — Production stoppage 6.5 hours

TIMELINE:
- 22:00: Routine check — vibration at 2.8 mm/s (elevated, no alarm)
- 02:30: Vibration alarm activated at 4.2 mm/s
- 02:45: Fan tripped on high vibration (>5.0 mm/s)
- 03:00: Engineer dispatched — confirmed bearing failure
- 06:00: Bearing replacement completed
- 08:30: Fan restarted — vibration normal 2.1 mm/s

ROOT CAUSE:
Primary: SKF 6315 bearing inner race fatigue failure
Contributing: Lubrication interval exceeded by 3 weeks (monthly schedule missed)
Contributing: Previous vibration spike (Feb 2024) not investigated

PRODUCTION IMPACT:
- Duration: 6.5 hours
- Blast Furnace production loss: ~650 tonnes
- Estimated cost: ₹1.8 crores

CORRECTIVE ACTIONS:
1. Implemented monthly lubrication reminder in SAP PM
2. Set vibration alarm threshold from 4.5 to 3.8 mm/s
3. Mandatory follow-up inspection after any vibration spike > 3.0 mm/s

LESSONS LEARNED:
Vibration trending over 7 days gave clear warning. If engineer had
analyzed the trend (2.1→2.4→2.6→2.8 mm/s over 10 days), bearing
replacement could have been planned — saving ~5 hours downtime.
